Marico's haircare portfolio spans the full price-point ladder from mass-market pure coconut oil to premium leave-in serums. When a question touches "hair oil", "coconut oil", "shampoo replacement", or any specific brand below, this portfolio map is the relevant context.

## Coconut oil — flagship

- **Parachute (Rigid Pack)** — pure coconut oil in the iconic blue rigid bottle. Mass-market, deeply rural, generally treated as a near-commodity by the consumer. The bedrock of Marico's volumes; growth here is incremental and tracks rural sentiment closely.
- **Parachute (Flexi / pouch)** — sachet and pouch formats for low-ticket, frequent purchase. Channel-skewed to general trade.
- **Parachute Coconut Crème** — premium coconut oil format with body cream / styling extension; modern-trade and e-commerce skewed.

## Value-added hair oils (VAHO)

A higher-margin, higher-growth segment than pure coconut. Marico has been deliberately premiumising the consumer up the ladder.

- **Parachute Advansed** — the umbrella value-added franchise. Active variants include **Aloe Vera**, **Jasmine**, **Coconut Crème**, **Onion** and **Ayurvedic**. Each variant targets a benefit cue (nourishment, scalp health, anti-hair-fall, fragrance, traditional formulations).
- **Nihar Naturals** — value-tier value-added oils. Variants include **Nihar Shanti Amla**, **Nihar Naturals Perfumed Coconut**, **Nihar Almond Hair Oil** and **Nihar Naturals Sarso Kesh Tel** (mustard-blend, regional). Heavily East-India and rural skewed; priced beneath Parachute Advansed.
- **Hair & Care** — non-sticky perfumed oil, urban / unisex positioning. Often a bridge SKU between coconut and leave-in styling.

## Premium and styling / treatment

- **Livon** — leave-in serum and conditioner. Distinctly urban, premium, modern-trade and e-commerce skewed.
- **Mediker** — anti-lice shampoo / oil, niche but durable franchise.
- **Mediker SafeLife** — line extension into hand wash / hygiene; small but listed in MIS.

## Patterns to keep in mind

- Volume is dominated by the rigid Parachute SKU; value growth disproportionately comes from the value-added oils and Livon.
- Seasonality: hair-oil consumption peaks in summer (cooling) and winter (nourishment); monsoon is typically softer for hair oils but stronger for anti-frizz and leave-ins.
- Pack-size mix shifts during inflationary periods — consumers downtrade to smaller SKUs without leaving the brand. A drop in volume per buyer is not necessarily a loss of franchise.
- Channel mix differs sharply: Parachute Rigid skews general trade and rural; Livon and the premium Parachute Advansed variants skew modern trade, e-commerce and quick commerce.
- "Hair oil" in the dataset can mean any of the above — when ambiguous, ask which sub-portfolio is in scope before slicing.
