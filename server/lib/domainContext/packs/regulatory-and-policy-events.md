Indian FMCG is a heavily-regulated sector. Step-changes in volumes or unit economics are sometimes explained not by demand or competition but by a regulatory change that took effect during the period. This pack lists the categories of policy events to keep in mind.

## Tax and duty

- **GST Council changes** — the GST slab (5%, 12%, 18%, 28%) for individual FMCG categories occasionally moves. A reclassification can change net realisation overnight; trade has to clear inventory at the old rate before the new rate kicks in, which compresses or expands a single quarter's reported numbers.
- **Edible-oil import duties** — the basic customs duty, agriculture infrastructure cess and IGST on imported palm, sunflower and soybean oils are adjusted regularly by the Ministry of Finance to manage food inflation. A 5–10 percentage-point duty change moves the entire edible-oil cost stack within weeks.
- **State-level VAT and entry taxes** (legacy) and state-specific cess on certain goods — relevant historically; mostly subsumed into GST.

## Labelling and product regulation

- **FSSAI labelling rules** — front-of-pack nutrition labelling, "high in fat / sugar / salt" disclosure, organic certification, fortification mandates. Change cycles take 6–18 months for full industry compliance.
- **Ingredient and additive bans** — periodic FSSAI / drug-controller actions on additives, colours, preservatives can force reformulation.
- **Health and cosmetics claims** (Drugs and Magic Remedies Act, Drugs and Cosmetics Act, ASCI codes) — claims like "anti-hair-fall", "boosts immunity", "ayurvedic" face scrutiny.

## Packaging and environment

- **Plastic Waste Management / Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)** rules — FMCG players have multi-year EPR obligations to take back / recycle plastic packaging tonnage. Compliance cost is real and rising.
- **Plastic ban on certain SKUs** — single-use plastics affect specific pack formats; sachets remain permitted but under scrutiny.

## Advertising and marketing

- **ASCI (Advertising Standards Council of India)** codes — claim substantiation, comparative advertising, surrogate advertising, influencer disclosures. Increasingly enforced.
- **Surrogate advertising rules** — relevant for adjacencies like deodorants in male-grooming (less for Marico but for category context).
- **Influencer marketing disclosure** — the Ministry of Consumer Affairs and ASCI mandate clear disclosure of paid endorsements; affects D2C / premium-personal-care marketing operating models.

## Trade and competition

- **Drug Pricing Control Order (DPCO)** — relevant if a category is notified (some FMCG-adjacent OTC items).
- **Competition Commission of India (CCI)** scrutiny on marketplace pricing parity, deep discounting and exclusivity arrangements with marketplaces — relevant for D2C / e-commerce strategy.

## Patterns to keep in mind

- A one-time spike or dip in a quarter's reported revenue might be a **trade-inventory adjustment** ahead of a known regulatory change (e.g. GST rate change).
- New FSSAI labelling rules often force pack-design refreshes that temporarily slow new-SKU launches.
- EPR compliance increases packaging cost in a slow, persistent way that shows up as gross-margin headwind even when commodity prices are stable.
- When attributing an unusual movement, ask "was there a regulatory change effective in this period?" before defaulting to brand or competitive explanations.
