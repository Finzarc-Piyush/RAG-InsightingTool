The Saffola franchise is Marico's second-largest pillar after Parachute and is being explicitly grown into a foods-and-nutrition house. It anchors the urban premium-health positioning of the company.

## Edible oils (Saffola)

- **Saffola Gold** — refined-blend cooking oil (rice-bran + safflower / sunflower) — the heart-health flagship.
- **Saffola Tasty** — refined-blend with corn oil, slightly value tier.
- **Saffola Total** — premium proposition centred on cardiac wellness.
- **Saffola Active** — refined-blend variant positioned around active lifestyles.
- **Saffola Aura** — extra-virgin olive oil; super-premium, urban, e-commerce-heavy.

Edible oils are commodity-exposed and pricing follows safflower, rice-bran, sunflower, palm and corn input cycles. Margin compression / expansion is largely a commodity story; share movements are smaller than price movements.

## Foods (Saffola brand extensions)

The "Foods" sub-portfolio extends Saffola's heart-health permission into adjacent better-for-you categories.

- **Saffola Masala Oats** — flavoured instant oats; the flagship foods SKU.
- **Saffola Oats** (plain) — entry-tier breakfast oats.
- **Saffola Honey** — packaged honey; competes squarely with Dabur Honey. Variants include classic and infused.
- **Saffola Mealmaker** — value-tier soya nuggets / textured vegetable protein.
- **Saffola Soya Chunks** — separately branded SKU, often shelved alongside Mealmaker.
- **Saffola Peanut Butter** — creamy and crunchy variants; competes with Pintola, Sundrop and MyFitness.
- **Saffola Munchiez** — better-for-you snacks (oat-based bites).
- **Saffola Choco-Almond Spread** — sweet spread adjacency.
- **Saffola FITTIFY** — premium nutrition (Hi-Protein meal shakes, gourmet soups, green coffee, weight-management).

## Other foods adjacencies

- **True Elements** — premium clean-label breakfast and snack brand (acquired). E-commerce / D2C native.
- **Plix** — plant-based nutrition and wellness D2C brand (acquired).
- **Coco Soul (cooking range)** — virgin coconut oil for cooking and wellness usage; sits at the intersection of foods and personal care.

## Patterns to keep in mind

- Foods has fundamentally different unit economics from haircare: longer NPD cycles, heavier A&P investment in early years, modern-trade and e-commerce skew, lower gross margins early but improving with scale.
- The "Saffola" brand equity carries heart-health / premium-health framing — variant launches that strain that equity (e.g. indulgent snacks) are scrutinised internally for fit.
- Quick commerce (Blinkit, Zepto, Instamart) is increasingly the marginal channel for foods — small basket, high frequency, urban, premium-skewed.
- A drop in Saffola edible-oil value alongside flat volumes typically reflects commodity-pass-through pricing, not demand weakness.
- "Foods" in MIS reports usually excludes edible oils; keep the two segments separate when interpreting growth rates.
