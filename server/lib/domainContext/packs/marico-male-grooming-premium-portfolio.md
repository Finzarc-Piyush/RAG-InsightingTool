Marico's premium personal-care and male-grooming portfolio is the most digitally-native part of the business. It is built largely through acquisition of D2C / digital-first brands and is the pillar most exposed to the quick-commerce and direct-to-consumer growth.

## Male grooming

- **Set Wet** — deodorant body sprays, hair gels and perfumes. Mass-premium positioning aimed at younger urban men. Heavily promoted around festive and college-season demand spikes.
- **Set Wet Studio X** — premium hair-styling sub-brand under Set Wet (gels, waxes, sprays); positioned for the more deliberate-styling consumer than the mass Set Wet line.
- **Beardo** — premium digital-first male grooming (beard oils, beard wash, hair growth, skincare for men). Acquired in stages and fully consolidated. Historically e-commerce native; now expanding into modern trade with curated SKU sets.

## Premium personal care (skincare, ayurveda, wellness)

- **Just Herbs** — premium ayurvedic personal-care (skincare, haircare). Acquired. Sells through D2C, modern trade and e-com.
- **Pure Sense** — D2C personal-care brand (body wash, hair, hand care) built on a "feel-good" sensorial proposition. Acquired.
- **Coco Soul** — virgin-coconut-oil based body / haircare and wellness range. Sits at the intersection of personal care and foods.
- **True Elements** — premium clean-label breakfast and snacks (full detail in the foods pack; counted under premium portfolio in segment reporting).
- **Plix** — plant-based nutrition and wellness D2C brand.
- **Other digital-first plays** — the company has been an active acquirer in this space; treat the list as fluid.

## Channel and unit-economics characteristics

- **Channel mix is very different** from Parachute / Saffola: D2C own websites, marketplaces (Amazon / Flipkart / Nykaa / Tata 1mg), quick commerce (Blinkit, Zepto, Instamart), and curated MT shelves. General trade is a small slice and growing slowly.
- **Marketing spend is performance-marketing-led** rather than mass TV. Customer acquisition cost (CAC), repeat rate and AOV (average order value) are the metrics that matter; share-of-shelf is less applicable.
- **Gross margin is generally higher** than the legacy portfolio at maturity; early years are heavy on customer acquisition spend.
- **Founder-led / acquired brands** often retain identity and creative independence after acquisition; Beardo and Just Herbs in particular run with a distinct voice.

## Patterns to keep in mind

- Quarter-on-quarter volatility is higher than the rest of the portfolio — performance marketing, festive sales, platform discount calendars (Big Billion Days, Great Indian Festival) and quick-commerce assortment changes all move the numbers more sharply than a typical FMCG SKU.
- Channel mix is the most informative secondary dimension — a "Beardo growth" question without a channel cut hides the story (D2C plateau vs. quick-commerce surge, etc.).
- Geographic skew is heavily metro / Tier 1; rural penetration is incidental.
- Repeat-rate and cohort metrics, when present in the dataset, are usually more informative than absolute revenue trends for these brands.
