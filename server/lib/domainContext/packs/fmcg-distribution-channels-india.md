Channel is the single most important secondary dimension in Indian FMCG analytics. Almost any question — share, growth, margin, mix — has a different answer once you cut by channel. This pack maps the channels and what each one signals.

## Channel taxonomy

- **General Trade (GT)** — kirana stores, paan-shops, chemist counters and traditional grocery. Largest channel by absolute value across most FMCG categories. Distribution is via wholesalers and direct-to-retailer (DTR) routes; the company-rolled-out distribution network is the moat. Outlet base is in the millions.
- **Modern Trade (MT)** — organised supermarket and hypermarket chains (Reliance Retail's Smart / Smart Bazaar, DMart, Spencer's, Star Bazaar, Lulu and others). Larger basket, longer shopping list, urban-skewed. Higher fill-rate and merchandising visibility, lower distributor margin.
- **E-commerce** — Amazon, Flipkart, BigBasket, Nykaa, 1mg / Tata 1mg. Slower delivery cycles than quick commerce, deeper SKU range, planned purchase. Strong for premium SKUs, large packs and brand-loyal repeat orders.
- **Quick commerce (Q-com)** — Blinkit, Zepto, Instamart, BB Now, Flipkart Minutes. 10–30 minute delivery from dark stores in metro and Tier-1 urban areas. Small basket, high frequency. The fastest-growing channel in FMCG; share-of-wallet is shifting toward Q-com from MT and convenience GT in metros. Limited SKU assortment per dark store.
- **Direct-to-Consumer (D2C)** — own-brand websites (e.g. beardo.in). Highest gross margin, full data ownership, useful for new-product launches and subscription. Volume share is small in absolute terms but informative for brand health.
- **Institutional / Out-of-home** — HORECA (hotels, restaurants, cafés), CSD (Canteen Stores Department, defence), institutional bulk. Specific to certain categories (edible oils, foods).

## Margin and ROI norms

- General trade typically operates on **lower retailer margin per unit** but very high volume; margins escalate down the value chain (distributor → wholesaler → retailer).
- Modern trade extracts **listing fees, slotting fees, merchandising spend** in exchange for shelf access — net margin to the brand is typically lower than GT.
- E-commerce and quick commerce demand **platform commissions, fulfilment fees, marketing spend** (sponsored listings) — net margin is variable per platform deal.
- D2C carries no platform tax but **CAC (customer acquisition cost) and fulfilment** can be high; mature D2C brands have the best brand-level margin.

## Patterns to keep in mind

- Premium SKUs disproportionately sell in MT, e-com and Q-com; mass SKUs disproportionately sell in GT.
- Quick-commerce growth is the most likely explanation for rapid recent shifts in metro mix.
- A drop in MT performance with stable Q-com growth typically means the retailer is reallocating shelf, not that the brand is weakening.
- "Channel skew" is a meaningful question to ask before drawing conclusions about brand strength.
