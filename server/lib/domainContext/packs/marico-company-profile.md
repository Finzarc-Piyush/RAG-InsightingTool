Marico Limited is one of India's leading consumer products companies, headquartered in Mumbai and listed on the BSE and NSE. The company operates in the beauty and wellness space, with deep roots in the Indian fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) sector and a growing footprint across emerging markets in South and South-East Asia, the Middle East and Africa.

## Business segments

Marico organises its portfolio into a small number of broad segments. The relative size of each segment shifts over time as the company premiumises and expands into adjacent categories, but the structural shape is stable.

- **Coconut oil and value-added hair oils** — the historical core, anchored by the Parachute and Parachute Advansed franchises and supported by mass brands such as Nihar Naturals and Hair & Care.
- **Saffola** — the edible oils and foods franchise, originally a heart-health refined-oil brand, now extended into oats, honey, soya chunks, peanut butter, breakfast cereals and premium nutrition (FITTIFY).
- **Premium personal care and male grooming** — Set Wet, Beardo, Just Herbs, True Elements, Plix and other recent acquisitions / D2C plays. Skewed to modern trade, e-commerce and quick commerce.
- **International business** — Bangladesh is the largest international market (Parachute and value-added hair oils dominate). Vietnam is second-largest, anchored by the **X-Men** male-grooming franchise (and Marino, Code 10, L'Ovité, Hair Code, Q1, Ozone, Vegy, Thuan Phat) under Marico South East Asia (MSEA, formerly ICP). MENA and South Africa are the smaller international markets.

## Strategic priorities (current era)

- **Diversification away from coconut-oil dependence** — Saffola Foods and the digital-first / premium personal-care portfolio are explicitly framed as growth engines. Management commentary regularly highlights the share of the portfolio that is "not Parachute".
- **Premiumisation across haircare** — moving the consumer up from rigid-pack pure coconut oil into value-added oils (Parachute Advansed Aloe Vera, Jasmine, Coconut Crème) at higher price points.
- **D2C and quick commerce** — house-of-brands strategy with several acquired digital-first brands (Beardo, Just Herbs, Plix, True Elements). Quick commerce is the fastest-growing channel and disproportionately important for premium SKUs.
- **Foods scale-up** — explicit ambition to grow the Foods franchise to a meaningful share of total revenue; new launches and category extensions appear regularly.
- **Operating leverage** — gross-margin sensitivity to copra, palm oil and crude derivatives is well known; pricing actions follow input-cost cycles with a 1–2 quarter lag.

## What this means for analysis

When interpreting Marico data, treat these segments as different businesses with different growth rates, margin profiles and channel mixes. A rural-skewed mass-haircare question and an urban premium grooming question may both be "Marico" but they live in different operating realities. Channel mix (general trade vs. modern trade vs. quick commerce vs. D2C) is often the most important secondary dimension after category.
