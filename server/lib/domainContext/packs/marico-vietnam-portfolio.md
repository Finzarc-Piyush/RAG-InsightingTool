Marico's Vietnam business is operated through Marico South East Asia Corporation (MSEA, formerly International Consumer Products — ICP, acquired in stages from 2011 onward). Vietnam is Marico's **second-largest international market after Bangladesh** and the company's beachhead into South-East Asia.

The Vietnam portfolio is structurally different from India: **male grooming is the largest pillar** (not coconut oil), and the historical brand strength sits in male personal care and home/kitchen care. When a Vietnam dataset is loaded, treat the brand mix, the channel reality and the seasonal calendar as distinct from India.

## Male grooming — the flagship pillar

- **X-Men** — flagship male grooming brand and the cornerstone of the Vietnam business. Holds a leading position in male shampoo, body wash and deodorant. Multiple variants and pack sizes across mass-premium price points.
- **X-Men For Boss** — premium line extension of X-Men. Higher price point, more sophisticated fragrance work, modern-trade and e-commerce skewed.
- **X-Men Aqua / X-Men Active / X-Men Power** — sub-variants under the X-Men umbrella, segmented by occasion (sport, daily, fresh, etc.).
- **Marino** — secondary male grooming brand; shower gel and personal-care SKUs at value-to-mass tier.
- **Code 10** — male hair styling and grooming line (pomade / brilliantine / styling cream); legacy brand with durable awareness.

## Personal care — women and family

- **L'Ovité** — premium women's personal care (skincare, body care).
- **Hair Code** — mass-tier haircare.
- **Q1** — value-tier personal care; high distribution in general trade.

## Home / kitchen / oral

- **Ozone** — oral care (toothpaste). Niche but durable franchise.
- **Vegy** — vegetable and produce cleanser; pioneer brand in the category in Vietnam.
- **Thuan Phat** — culinary / sauces (fish sauce, soy sauce, condiments). Inherited from the ICP foods business; serves both retail and HORECA.

## Channel mix in Vietnam

- **General Trade** — small format grocery and convenience; still the largest channel, similar to India but with a higher density per square kilometre in HCMC and Hanoi.
- **Modern Trade** — Aeon, Lotte Mart, Co.opmart, MM Mega Market, Bach Hoa Xanh, Big C / GO!, WinMart. Heavier presence in HCMC and Hanoi than secondary cities.
- **E-commerce** — Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop. TikTok Shop in particular has been the fastest-growing channel for premium personal-care SKUs.
- **Quick commerce / convenience-format chains** — GS25, Circle K, FamilyMart, 7-Eleven, Bach Hoa Xanh. Distinct dynamics from India quick commerce — convenience-store presence is much larger.
- **Pharmacies** — relevant for personal-care SKUs (shampoo, body wash, oral care) more than in India.
- **HORECA / institutional** — relevant specifically for Thuan Phat sauces.

## Competitive set in Vietnam

The Vietnam-only competitive set is materially different from India — most India-only competitors do not operate at meaningful scale in Vietnam, and vice versa.

- **Male grooming** — Unilever Vietnam (Clear Men, Axe, Dove Men+Care, Lifebuoy Men), P&G Vietnam (Old Spice, Gillette, Head & Shoulders Men), Romano (Wipro Unza), Nivea Men, Enchanteur, Bvlgari Man, Adidas (men's care).
- **Women's personal care** — Pond's (Unilever), Olay (P&G), L'Oréal Paris, Pantene, Dove, Sunsilk, Clear, Essance, Hada Labo, Senka.
- **Sauces (Thuan Phat competition)** — Masan Consumer (Chinsu, Nam Ngu fish sauce, Tam Thai Tu), Phu Quoc fish-sauce makers, Knorr (Unilever), Maggi (Nestlé).
- **Oral care** — Colgate-Palmolive Vietnam, P/S (Unilever), Sensodyne (GSK), Closeup, Twin Lotus.
- **Vegetable cleansers** — Lipton-style entrants from Unilever, smaller domestic brands.

## Seasonality and demand events

- **Tet (Lunar New Year, Jan–Feb)** — the equivalent of Diwali in India and the **largest single demand event** of the year in Vietnam. Gifting, premium SKU pull-through, big trade load-ins 4–6 weeks ahead, and channel inventories managed tightly through and after Tet.
- **Vietnamese summer (May–Aug)** — peak shower-gel, deodorant and X-Men body-wash demand.
- **Mid-Autumn Festival / Tet Trung Thu (Sep–Oct)** — gifting pulse for personal care and confectionery-adjacent categories.
- **Black Friday / Online Friday / 11.11 / 12.12** — e-commerce / TikTok Shop discount events that disproportionately move premium and D2C SKUs.

## Patterns to keep in mind

- "Marico Vietnam" mix is dominated by **male grooming**, not by coconut oil or edible oils. A "company growth in Vietnam" question should default to X-Men, X-Men For Boss, Marino and Code 10 as the core, with L'Ovité, Hair Code and Q1 as the secondary cluster.
- **Clear Men (Unilever)** is X-Men's biggest direct competitor; share questions in the male shampoo / body wash category should expect Unilever as the main rival.
- **TikTok Shop** is an outsized channel in Vietnam compared to India — premium personal-care SKUs increasingly index on TikTok-Shop performance.
- Channel mix and Tet timing are the two most informative secondary cuts for any Vietnam analytical question.
- Vietnamese region cuts that frequently appear in MSEA reports: HCMC (South), Hanoi (North), Da Nang / Central, plus Mekong Delta and Highlands. These are NOT interchangeable with India region codes.
