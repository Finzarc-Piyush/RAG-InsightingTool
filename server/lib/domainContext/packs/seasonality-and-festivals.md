Indian FMCG demand is **highly seasonal**. Year-on-year (YoY) is almost always the right comparison; sequential month-on-month or quarter-on-quarter movements often reflect calendar shifts rather than business reality. This pack maps the meaningful seasonal patterns for Marico's portfolio.

## Quarterly demand pattern (Indian fiscal year, April–March)

- **Q1 (Apr–Jun)** — peak summer. Hair-oil and cooling-oil demand rises (especially light, perfumed hair oils, after-bath leave-ins). Cooling foods (oats, summer recipes). Eid (variable, often in this quarter). Closes the school exam / wedding-season cycle.
- **Q2 (Jul–Sep)** — monsoon. Hair-oil demand softens; anti-frizz, leave-in conditioner and dandruff-control formats benefit. Onam in Kerala drives a meaningful pulse for coconut and edible oil; Raksha Bandhan, Janmashtami, Ganesh Chaturthi follow. Rural consumption is monsoon-sensitive — a poor monsoon shows up as a dip in rural FMCG categories two quarters out.
- **Q3 (Oct–Dec)** — festive peak. Diwali, Dussehra, Karwa Chauth, Bhai Dooj, Christmas. Largest quarter for many categories — gifting, premium SKU pull, deodorant gifting (Set Wet), male-grooming festive promos, urban modern-trade packs. Foods sees a meaningful winter pull (oats, honey, premium nutrition).
- **Q4 (Jan–Mar)** — wedding season + winter. Hair-oil and styling demand benefits from wedding occasions. Year-end clearance and Republic Day promotions in MT and e-com. Channel inventories are managed tightly into year-end.

## Festivals — explicit demand triggers

- **Diwali (Oct–Nov)** — the biggest commercial event of the year. Gifting, premium SKU pull, festive packaging, modern-trade promotions. Plan-and-load by trade often happens 4–6 weeks ahead — so primary sales (sell-in) peak before secondary sales (sell-out).
- **Onam (Aug–Sep, Kerala)** — significant for coconut-oil franchises and edible oils in Kerala. Concentrated regional pull.
- **Pongal / Sankranti (Jan)** — South India, edible oils and traditional sweets ingredients.
- **Eid (variable)** — gifting and male-grooming pull, especially in North and West.
- **Holi (Mar)** — colour-and-stain category implications, but limited direct portfolio impact.
- **Rakhi (Aug)** — gifting pull for premium personal care and male grooming.

## Weather and agricultural cycles

- **Hair-oil consumption** — typically peaks in summer (cooling) and winter (nourishment); softer in monsoon.
- **Cooling-oil seasonality** is concentrated and short — May–July in most of India.
- **Edible-oil consumption** — relatively flat across the year, but festivals concentrate large family / traditional cooking and amplify demand.
- **Rural demand** — monsoon-sensitive; a normal-to-good monsoon reflects in volumes 1–2 quarters later.

## Patterns to keep in mind

- A "weak Q3" or "strong Q3" in primary sales might just be a calendar shift — Diwali moving 10 days year-on-year materially changes when sell-in happens.
- Always prefer YoY same-quarter comparisons for seasonal categories; month-on-month rarely tells the truth.
- Pay attention to **festive timing** when comparing two years — a 10-day shift in Diwali can move ~15% of festive volumes between quarters.
- For Marico specifically, **Onam and Diwali** are the two festivals most commonly named in management commentary.
