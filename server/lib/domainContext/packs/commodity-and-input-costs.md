Marico's gross margin is sensitive to a handful of agricultural and industrial commodities. When a question is about margin movement, cost of goods sold (COGS), pricing actions or value-vs-volume divergence, the input-cost cycle is usually a load-bearing part of the answer.

## Key inputs

- **Copra (dried coconut)** — primary raw material for Parachute coconut oil. Sourced from Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Kerala, and Andhra Pradesh. Prices follow the coconut harvest cycle and are influenced by drought, cyclone and procurement-policy shifts.
- **Refined safflower / kardi** — primary input for Saffola Gold blend.
- **Rice-bran oil, sunflower oil, corn oil** — Saffola refined-blend inputs; trades on global vegetable-oil prices and import duty.
- **Crude palm oil (CPO) and palm kernel oil** — affects packaging via derivative chemicals (HDPE) and certain blended SKUs. Indonesian export policy materially moves palm prices.
- **Aroma chemicals, essential oils, fragrances** — for value-added hair oils (Parachute Advansed Aloe Vera, Jasmine etc.) and male-grooming perfumes.
- **Crude derivatives** — HDPE / PP for rigid bottles, films and packaging laminates. Tracks Brent and naphtha cracker spreads.
- **Glass and aluminium** — premium personal-care packaging.

## How input-cost cycles transmit to results

- **Lag from commodity to gross margin** is typically **1–2 quarters** — driven by inventory of finished goods, fixed-price contracts, and pricing decisions on shelf.
- **Pass-through pricing** is the standard playbook: when input costs rise sharply, brands raise MRP or shrink pack size (grammage); when costs fall, brands hold price and let margin expand for a quarter or two before passing back to the consumer.
- **Saffola edible oils** are the most commodity-pass-through-heavy line in Marico's portfolio — value movements often reflect price changes rather than volume changes.
- **Parachute coconut oil** has historically been managed with disciplined pricing; copra price spikes get passed through but with a lag.
- **Foods and premium personal care** are less commodity-exposed in the short term — promotional spend and channel investment are the bigger margin drivers.

## Macro and FX context

- **INR/USD** — most imported edible oils (palm, sunflower) are priced in USD. Rupee depreciation pressures landed-cost economics for refined-blend oils.
- **Government import-duty changes** on edible oils are recurring; a single duty change can shift category cost structures by several percentage points overnight.
- **Monsoon quality** affects copra harvest and rural disposable income simultaneously — both supply-side (input cost) and demand-side (consumer ability to pay) are touched.
- **Crude / Brent prices** flow through to packaging cost and rural transport / freight cost.

## Patterns to keep in mind

- A sudden gross-margin swing 1–2 quarters after a sharp commodity move is consistent with the standard lag.
- A value-volume divergence in Saffola edible oils almost always has a price-pass-through explanation.
- A sustained Parachute volume softness during a copra price spike usually means consumers are downtrading to loose / unbranded oil, not switching to a competitor.
- When attributing margin movement, separate **commodity** (cyclical, mean-reverting) from **mix** (premiumisation, channel) and **operating leverage** (scale benefits) — they have different durations.
